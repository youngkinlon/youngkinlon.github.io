Research Experience

BRACE: Taming Sharp Irregularities via Barycentric Rational Forecasting for Fast DiT Inference
  • Proposed BRACE, a training-free acceleration framework for Diffusion Transformers, to address the high inference cost of DiT models and the instability of existing long-step prediction methods. By combining a local sliding window, barycentric rational extrapolation, and adaptive Chebyshev weighting, BRACE achieved quality-efficiency trade-off than prior methods on DiT-XL/2, FLUX.1-dev, and HunyuanVideo.
  • First-author paper submitted to ACM MM 2026.
FADE: Fractional Anomalous Dynamics Extrapolation for Diffusion Transformers Acceleration
  • By characterizing DiT latent trajectories as anomalous diffusion, FADE utilizes a Mittag-Leffler fractional operator to capture complex feature evolution. This overcomes the limitations of traditional integer-order operators under high-curvature dynamics, enabling accurate extrapolation of non-linear trajectories while mitigating error accumulation. Furthermore, by exploiting the cross-sample stability of these dynamics, we construct a multi-dimensional Look-Up Table (LUT) to achieve zero-overhead, spatiotemporally adaptive parameter retrieval.
  • First-author paper submitted to NeurIPS 2026.
HDS: Heterogeneity-Driven Safeguarding for Structure-Aware DiT Acceleration
  • Proposed the HDS framework to overcome homogeneous layer assumptions in DiT acceleration. By profiling inter-layer sensitivity online via a novel Heterogeneity Score (HS), HDS selectively safeguards vulnerable layers while accelerating robust ones, improving the fidelity-efficiency Pareto frontier with negligible overhead.
  • Co-first-author paper submitted to NeurIPS 2026.
GRAD-RIR: Topology-Aware Graph-Conditioned Diffusion Model for Sparse RIR Prediction
  • Studied real room RIR estimation under sparse measurements, geometric occlusion, and strong spatial variation. Constructed a topological relational graph to model spatial constraints, incorporated visual cues to capture complex patterns, and designed a conditional generative framework for RIR reconstruction, improving estimation accuracy and spatial generalization with SOTA results on multiple acoustic metrics.
  • Co-first-author paper submitted to ACM MM 2026.
